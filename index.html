Dharma materials find new homes.

Books, practice texts, and ritual objects, passed with care from one generation to the next.

Longtime practitioners accumulate sacred materials: books, malas, ritual objects, thangkas. When it’s time to pass them on, they want them to reach people who will truly use them. At the same time, newer practitioners are searching for exactly these materials and can’t always find or afford them.

FES bridges that gap.

How It Works

Receive

Donors entrust us with their dharma materials, knowing we’ll honor their wishes, including restrictions on vajrayana texts.

Organize

We carefully sort, catalog, and store each item, from rare practice texts to beautiful ritual objects, treating everything with the respect it deserves.

Give

Practitioners visit the FES “treasure room” and find what they need: books, malas, and ritual implements, all offered freely to support their path.

Your Donations at Work

The Treasure Room

Practitioners browse tables covered with donated dharma materials. People discover the exact book they need, receive a mala for their practice, or find a ritual object they couldn’t have obtained on their own. Every item was once cherished by someone else, and now it supports a new practitioner’s journey.

Dharma Behind Bars

Buddhist Chaplain Lasette Brown uses books donated by FES to stock the Philosophy & Non-Christian Religion section of the Adams County Jail library. Incarcerated practitioners now have access to teachings that can genuinely change their lives.

Completed Programs
  1. Seminary Transcript Drive (2024 – 2025): We received and joyfully delivered many boxes of Vajradhatu Seminary transcripts to participants in the 3 Yanas Retreat at Drala Mountain Center. We plan to do this again in 2026!
  2. Grief on the Path of Dharma (2024): A hybrid program on love, loss, and grief featuring Miriam Tarcov, Jon Barbieri, Bryan Welch, and Colin Stubbert, moderated by Judy Lief. Attended by about 100 people.
  3. Abhisheka Preparation (2023–2024): We helped students prepare for the Vajrayogini Abhisheka given by Ringu Tulku in Halifax in 2024. FES arranged reading transmissions, ngöndro practice, and provided fellowship during the challenging work of the preliminaries.
  4. Rabjam Rinpoche Visit (2023): FES ran the security and venue team for Rabjam Rinpoche's 2023 visit to Boulder, in cooperation with Rimé Society and Naropa University. He bestowed empowerments for the Sadhana of Mahamudra, Gesar, Guyagarbha and Vajrakilaya. FES provided training for several volunteers and hosted around 600 participants.
