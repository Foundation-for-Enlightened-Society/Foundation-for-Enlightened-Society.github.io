Who: a non-profit based in Boulder, run by younger practitioners in the lineages of Chögyam Trungpa.

Goal: to pass on these linages to future generations.

How: by supporting advanced training and teaching opportunities for newer students.

Advised by: Judy Lief, Larry Mermelstein, Valerie Lorig, Jon Barbieri.

We serve: everyone who supports these goals, but especially newer and/or younger practitioners.

Major Initiatives

  1. Shambhala Training Directors Training, led by Jon Barbieri and Valerie Lorig.
  2. Receiving, organizing, and passing on dharma materials, especially from older practitioners who do not need them anymore to newer folks who do. Contact donations@fes.ngo to donate.
  3. Establish a residential practice center to intensify training for residents and give visitors a taste of a Shambhala Household; inspired by Marpa House and KCCL.

Recurring Events

  1. Warrior Soirée social event (in Boulder - open to all)
  2. Ngöndro practice (hybrid, requires pointing out instructions)
  3. Werma Sadhana practice (hybrid, requires reading transmission)
  4. Vajrayogini practice (in person, requires abhisheka)

To learn about our events, please sign up for our mailing list to receive notifications about events or contact practice@fes.ngo.

Recent Initiatives

  1. Grief on the Path of Dharma: this was a hybrid program, held at the Boulder Shambhala Center about love, loss, death and grief featuring Miriam Tarcov, Jon Barbieri, Bryan Welch, and Colin Stubbert, moderated by Judy Lief. It was attended by about 100 people.
  2. Seminary Transcript program: We received many boxes of Vajradhatu Seminary transcripts and delievered them to participants in the 3 Yanas Retreat at Drala Mountain Center, where they were joyfully received by participants.
  3. Abhisheka preparation: We helped students prepare for the 2024 Vajrayogini Abhisheka in Halifax with Ringu Tulku by arranging reading transmissions, hosting group ngöndro practice, and generally providing fellowship during the challenging work of the preliminaries.
  4. Rabjam Rinpoche venue team: We ran the security and venue team for Rabjam Rinpoche's 2023 visit to Boulder (hosted by Rime Society), providing training for about 10 volunteers and hosting around 600 guests. Through this program we helped to pass on the tradition of mindful protection of visiting dignitaries and guests.
